Gene Information

Base (MC1R)

The MC1R gene controls whether an animal produces black (eumelanin) or red (pheomelanin) pigment. This is the primary determinant of base coat color.

  • ED (Dominant Black): Forces production of black pigment. Even one copy will result in a black-based coat color. This allele is dominant over both E+ and e.
  • E+ (Wild-type Extension): Allows normal pigment expression. The actual color depends on other genes like ASIP. This is the "normal" allele.
  • e (Recessive Red): Prevents black pigment production entirely, resulting in red-based colors only. Two copies (e/e) are needed to express this trait.

Dilution (PMEL)

The PMEL gene dilutes or lightens whatever base color is present. The Highland dilution (D) acts in a dose-dependent manner—more copies mean more dilution.

  • d (Non-dilute): No dilution effect. Colors appear at full intensity.
  • D (Highland Dilution): Lightens the coat color. One copy (d/D) produces medium dilution (Black → Dun, Red → Yellow). Two copies (D/D) produce strong dilution (Black → Silver, Red → White).

Agouti (ASIP)

The ASIP gene modifies pigment distribution to create patterns. In Highland cattle, it controls brindle striping and bùs dubh (black muzzle) traits.

  • A (Non-agouti): Produces solid, uniform color without patterning or dark points.
  • A+ (Wild-type Agouti): Creates bùs dubh - characteristic black pigmentation on the muzzle, ear tips, and points. Only visible on red-based cattle (e/e or E+ without ED). On black cattle (ED), this trait is masked.
  • Abr (Brindle): Creates vertical striping pattern by restricting black pigment to bands. Only visible when paired with E+ (not with ED or e/e), resulting in the distinctive Brindle color.
