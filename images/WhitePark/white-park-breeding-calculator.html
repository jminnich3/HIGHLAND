About White Park Cattle Breeding

White Park cattle have a simple genetics system for point color, controlled by the MC1R (Extension) gene. All White Park cattle have the characteristic white body with colored points.

The point color is determined by whether the animal has the dominant ED (black points) or recessive e (red points) allele. Black-pointed cattle are more common, while red-pointed cattle are a rarer variant.

This calculator shows the probabilities of offspring phenotypes based on the phenotypes or genotypes of both parents.
