About Shorthorn Cattle Breeding

Shorthorn cattle have simple codominant color genetics. The Red (R) and White (W) alleles are codominant, meaning neither is dominant over the other.

Breeding Outcomes:

  • Red × Red = 100% Red offspring
  • White × White = 100% White offspring
  • Red × White = 100% Roan offspring
  • Roan × Roan = 25% Red, 50% Roan, 25% White
  • Red × Roan = 50% Red, 50% Roan
  • White × Roan = 50% White, 50% Roan

This makes Shorthorn color genetics highly predictable and is a classic example of codominance taught in biology classes worldwide.
