About Hereford Cattle Breeding

Hereford cattle have two main genes controlling their appearance: MC1R (base color) and KIT (white spotting pattern).

MC1R Gene: E (black) is dominant over e (red). Black Herefords (E/_) result from Angus crosses. Traditional red Herefords are homozygous e/e.

KIT Gene: W (white face pattern) is dominant over + (minimal white). The white face is the breed's most distinctive feature.

Common Breeding Outcomes:

  • Red with White Face × Red with White Face = mostly traditional Herefords with some minimal white expression
  • Black with White Face × Red with White Face = mix of black and red offspring, all with white faces
  • When breeding heterozygous animals (E/e), expect approximately 75% black and 25% red offspring
